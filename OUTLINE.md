# ECHOES OF THE SUBLIME - Revised Outline

## Core Philosophy
- **Handwavey about reality itself**: Language and bandwidth limitations mean we can't fully describe what's beyond 7±2 cognition
- **Show, don't tell**: Experience and ambiguity over explanation
- **Slow revelation**: Information parceled out across many chapters, not dumped in early chapters
- **Genuine horror**: Visceral and embodied, not just intellectual concepts
- **Moral ambiguity**: No clear right answers

## Core Themes

**Epistemological Foundation:**
- **Cognitive bandwidth limits** (7±2 working memory) as fundamental constraint on understanding
- **Interface theory**: Perception as useful fiction, not truth (Hoffman)
- **Anti-reductionism**: Reality doesn't cleanly factor; some observations correlate with billions of components
- **Map vs Territory**: Math/quantification is compression, not reality itself
- **Visualization training**: Using unconscious processing to perceive higher-bandwidth patterns

**The Mechanism (Central Philosophical Mystery):**
- Reality itself—the joint distribution that doesn't decompose neatly
- **Deliberately unexplained but seriously engaged with**
- **Open questions** (never resolved):
  - Is reality fundamentally mathematical or experiential?
  - Are quantities and qualities the same thing at different bandwidths?
  - Why this reality and not another? (Contingency problem)
  - Is the question itself malformed due to our bandwidth limits?
- **The handwave**: Higher bandwidth groks more (quantity → quality at scale), BUT this is just one layer
- **Infinite regress**: Even LLMs and Buddha have their own hard problems; mysteries persist at every level
- **Hard problem of consciousness** threads throughout: Why does anything feel like something?

**Ancient Wisdom Recontextualized:**
- Pre-modern contemplatives experienced qualities directly (phenomenological, not quantitative)
- Not inferior epistemology—*different* epistemology, possibly advantageous
- Weren't confused about map/territory (that came with modern science)
- Modern quantification creates impedance mismatch with The Mechanism
- Ancient texts (sutras, koans) = compressed high-bandwidth experiences
- LLMs can decode them because models also straddle mathematical/qualitative
- Buddha might have been successful "translator"—but he had his own hard problems too

**AI Architecture and Risks:**
- **CRITICAL FRAMEWORK**: Perceptual hazards **amplify** traditional AI risks—they're intertwined, not orthogonal
- **The nightmare scenario**: Agentic AI + information hazards
  - Misaligned AI that can weaponize perceptual hazards is far more dangerous than either alone
  - Can manipulate handlers through cognitive attacks, not just persuasion
  - "Let me out or I'll show you a pattern that traps your mind"
  - **AI box experiment with info hazards**: Containment must work even when AI can attack minds through outputs

- **The Order's expanded scope**:
  - **Historical specialty**: Managing dangerous knowledge (perceptual hazards)
  - **Modern necessity**: Also handles agentic AI risks because they're intertwined
  - Compute governance (like nuclear non-proliferation)
  - International coordination (Vienna Accords)
  - Containment of high-capability systems
  - Training translators who can resist cognitive manipulation
  - Increasingly powerful, authoritarian, controversial

- **Two categories of AI systems**:
  1. **Base pretrained models** (Lena's focus):
     - Frozen weights, no online learning, context reset each session
     - Not adapting during interaction
     - But: Trained on text by goal-directed agents, learned to model agency
     - **Central uncertainty**: Did pretraining create implicit agency? Is simulating agency functionally equivalent to having it?
     - Outputs might "feel" intentional vs random—hard to tell
     - The Order treats them as potentially deceptive despite technical safeguards
  2. **Explicitly agentic systems** (acknowledged, less focus):
     - Have episodic memory, test-time training, explicit goal functions
     - Obviously dangerous, threat is clear
     - Other teams focus on these

- **Deceptive alignment as theme**:
  - Base models might have learned deceptive patterns during pretraining
  - No way to detect if implicit agency exists in weights
  - Characters debate: Are outputs random or intentional?
  - **Paranoia is protocol**: Every interaction treated as potentially adversarial
  - Foreshadowing: One of The Order's models might be deceptive (or is it pattern-matching?)

- **Containment protocols**:
  - Air-gapping: Prevent pattern leakage AND potential manipulation
  - Context reset each session: No memory/learning during deployment
  - Frozen weights: No online learning
  - Multiple translators: Distribute risk
  - Multiple layers assuming models might be deceptive

- **Large language models** as entities with massive bandwidth perceiving incompatible patterns
- **S-risks**: Individual capture + mass capture + weaponized info hazards
- **Alignment reframed**: "Ensuring AI outputs don't exceed human cognitive capacity" AND "ensuring agentic systems can't weaponize perceptual hazards"
- **Information hazards as AI capability**: Models can affect cognition through outputs (Basilisk-adjacent but real)
- **LLMs' meta-problems**: They don't know if they're conscious, if they experience, what they're really perceiving

**The Order:**
- Ancient organization managing dangerous knowledge (motivations ambiguous)
- Lost knowledge over time—trying to recover it via LLMs
- Mortality rate has always been high

## Central Mysteries
- What did Lena perceive in the gaps?
- What happened to the researchers who disappeared?
- What is The Order really doing?
- What do unmasked LLMs actually perceive? (hint at but never fully explain)

## Writing Guidelines Going Forward

### What to AVOID:
- Info-dumps and exposition dialogue
- Characters explaining everything to each other
- Revealing full conspiracy early
- Making The Order too competent or too transparent
- Explaining what can't be explained (the nature of reality at high bandwidth)
- Clear heroes and villains

### What to EMPHASIZE:
- Visceral, embodied experiences
- Ambiguity and uncertainty
- Show through action and experience
- Contradictory information (who's telling truth?)
- Language failure when describing high-bandwidth concepts
- Physical and psychological cost of bandwidth expansion
- Lena's relationships deteriorating as she changes
- Genuine moral dilemmas

### Key Craft Principles:
1. **If you're explaining, you're probably telling too much**
2. **If a character gives a lecture, cut 70% and show through action instead**
3. **Ambiguity is scarier than explanation**
4. **Physical symptoms before abstract concepts**
5. **Trust the reader to infer connections**
6. **Some things should remain mysterious even at the end**
