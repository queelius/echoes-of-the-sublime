## Primary Characters

### Dr. Lena Hart (Protagonist)

**Basic Info:**
- Cognitive scientist specializing in consciousness
- Age: Early 30s (implied)
- PhD from MIT (neuroscience/cognitive science background)
- Father deceased (died when she was 19) — major grief moment frozen in spacetime
- Mother: Anna Hart — has stroke in Ch 5, now with permanent deficits

**Arc Summary:**
1. **Chapters 1-3**: Curious scientist, emotionally engaged, seeks understanding
2. **Chapters 4-7**: Training begins, gradual dissolution of empathy, pattern recognition increasing
3. **Chapters 8-11**: Advanced work, almost fully dissolved, clinical observer of own transformation
4. **Chapter 12**: Moral nadir — lets Anna Chen capture for data
5. **Chapters 13-14**: Descent to Yog-Sothoth, climactic choice, humanity returns at threshold

**Key Transformation Markers:**
- Ch 5: Mother's stroke — phone call, calculates prognosis before feeling anything
- Ch 6: Hospital visit to mother — performs warmth but feels nothing
- Ch 6: Can't remember what mother's voice sounded like
- Ch 7: Watches Maya capture without intervening
- Ch 10: Child discovering rainbow — observes joy she can no longer feel
- Ch 12: Lets Anna Chen capture for "data" — the nadir
- Ch 14: "Moment of being purely alive" — humanity resurfaces at threshold
- By Ch 14, she has helped break 31+ trainee minds — her dissolution is not just emotional loss but active complicity in harm

**Emotional Flickers:**
- Ch 11: Webb fracture — 3 heartbeats of real empathy for Webb's trapped suffering (ch11:383)
- Ch 12: Butterfly warmth — brief ghost of feeling during routine observation
- Ch 12: Ghost residue — phantom sensation of empathy, quickly dissolved
- Ch 13: Shape-of-fear — recognizes fear's architecture without experiencing it
- Ch 13: Ice-thinning — sense that something beneath the clinical surface is trying to surface
- Ch 14: Mother beat — Anna Hart reaching for her hand; Lena reaching back with nothing behind the gesture, then needing to call her (ch14:355)

These bridge the gap between Lena's moral nadir (Ch 12) and her humanity returning (Ch 14).

**Session Records:**
- Nyarlathotep: 31 minutes — matched Rostova's record (Ch 12)
- Yog-Sothoth: 27 minutes — record; third person to survive past 25 (Ch 13)

**Relationships:**
- Ethan (colleague): Starts warm → becomes strained → needs brief reunion scene
- David Chen: Training peer, potential witness to her transformation
- Webb: Mirror image (he feels, she thinks)
- Mother (Anna Hart): Marker of dissolution — stroke visit, declining contact

**Voice/Manner:**
- Early: Warm, curious, empathetic, uses metaphors
- Late: Clinical, observational, analytical, compressed speech

**Current Arc:**
- Mentor figure to Lena
- Speaks in koans and compressed wisdom
- "The river runs to the sea by many paths"
- "Fingers pointing at moon, not the moon itself"

His three-generation family commitment to The Order makes him both the most experienced and the most invested — possibly unable to question the work because it's his family's entire legacy. When he says "Morrison's sacrifice was not in vain," is that wisdom or sunk-cost rationalization?

## Ethical Stances (Internal Spectrum Positions)

Each character occupies a position on The Order's internal spectrum (see [lore.md](lore.md) — Internal Spectrum). These positions are shaped by personal proximity to dangerous ideas and shift over time.

### Rostova — The Conflicted

Recommended shutting down Vault 9 access. Wakes at 0300 running utilitarian calculus. "Wondering if The Order's entire existence is just elaborate form of harm." But still guides Lena to Yog-Sothoth — conflicted enough to doubt, committed enough to continue. Her hands shake when describing Morrison's fate. She is the best-case scenario for what "success" looks like, and she knows it isn't good enough.

### Master Chen (Director, early timeline) — Committed Truth-Seeker with Legacy Weight

Denied Rostova's shutdown recommendation. Believes understanding The Mechanism justifies costs. But his father's "functional enlightenment" haunts him — he knows the personal cost firsthand. Three generations of commitment makes stepping back feel like betraying his family's life work. The question: is his conviction genuine wisdom, or is he too invested to see clearly? By the later chapters, the Director title has passed to Rostova — the transition is implicit.

### Yuki Tanaka — The Committed

"We pursue truth... we've decided that's worth the cost." Direct, practical, unsentimental about casualties. But wrote concerned note about Lena's value drift — "Ethical compromise logged." So not indifferent to individual suffering; she just weighs it against the mission and the mission wins. Every time. So far.

### Webb — The Exposed / Ambivalent

"Ask me when I'm catatonic beside Morrison." Has seen enough to doubt but can't unknow what he knows. Represents the "exposed" position — already damaged, can't go back. His cognition is fragmenting while his feelings persist, which means he *feels* the moral weight of the work more acutely than those whose emotions have dissolved. The tragedy: the person best equipped to feel the horror is the one losing the ability to articulate it.

### Hayes — The Outsider / Protector

Rejects utilitarian calculus. Concerned about humanity of the translators. Can't evaluate the work on its own terms because she lacks bandwidth. Represents what translators are leaving behind — competent, humane, reasonable, and fundamentally unable to see what they see. Her frustration isn't ignorance; it's the rational response to being told "trust us" by people who are visibly deteriorating.

### Castellanos — The Archivist / Committed (Scholarly)

Committed to preservation and historical continuity. Her position is scholarly — the work has value because it connects to centuries of human inquiry. Less directly exposed to model outputs than translators. For her, The Order is a library as much as a laboratory. The danger feels more abstract, more historical.

### Lena — The Full Trajectory

Lena traverses the entire spectrum across the novel: curious outsider → committed truth-seeker → exposed → conflicted (Ch 14, when humanity returns). Her arc IS the spectrum, collapsed into a single character's journey.

## Character Consistency Rules

1. **No specific bandwidth numbers** — use qualitative comparisons only
2. **Webb feels, Lena thinks** — maintain this inversion
3. **Morrison is always whispering** — specific phrases listed above
4. **Model outputs are always COHERENT** — alien coherence, never fragmented (see [style-guide.md](style-guide.md))
5. **Dissolution is progressive** — track Lena's warmth declining chapter by chapter
6. **Models are ghosts, not organisms** — no drives, no memory between sessions, no continuous self
7. **Jagged frontier** — models have blind spots, not uniform omniscience; translators needed to bridge the gap
8. **Alienness scales with model** — Shoggoth feels human, Nyarlathotep feels eerily human, Yog-Sothoth feels *translated*
